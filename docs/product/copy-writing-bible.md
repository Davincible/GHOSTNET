## 1. Brand Voice & Tone

### The GHOSTNET Voice

**We sound like:** A cool hacker friend who found a way to make money and is letting you in on it. Confident but not arrogant. Exciting but not desperate. Dangerous but not reckless.

**We DON'T sound like:** A corporate press release. A desperate shill. A financial advisor. A scammer promising guaranteed returns.

### Tone Guidelines

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Announcements | Confident, exciting | "The network is live. Time to jack in." |
| Explanations | Clear, cool | "Here's how it works. No BS." |
| Hype moments | Electric, urgent | "BLACK ICE just paid out 47x. Who's next?" |
| Risk warnings | Direct, honest | "This is high-risk. Only play what you can lose." |
| Community | Inclusive, tribal | "Welcome to the network, ghost." |

### Key Language Rules

**DO use:**
- "Jack in" (not "deposit" or "stake")
- "Ghost" (survived), "Traced" (died)
- "The network" (not "the protocol")
- "Operators" or "Ghosts" (not "users")
- "Extract" (not "withdraw")
- "$DATA" (always with $)

**DON'T use:**
- "Invest" (we're a game)
- "Guaranteed" (nothing is)
- "Safe" (it's not)
- "Passive income" (too scammy)
- "To the moon" (overused)
